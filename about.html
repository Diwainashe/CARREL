About

Carrel Kumbirai is a wildlife painter whose work celebrates Africa’s iconic species through warm, earth-toned realism. Each piece is built with patience, detail, and a deep respect for the landscapes and animals that inspire it.

Artist statement

Carrel’s paintings explore the quiet power of wildlife — the alert gaze of a leopard, the calm presence of a lion, or the expressive character of the painted dog. His style leans into natural earth tones to echo the feeling of savanna light, dust, bark, and stone.

Many works begin with reference moments gathered through conversations and collaboration with African wildlife photographers. Those images become a starting point — not a shortcut — guiding anatomy, texture, and mood while allowing the final painting to carry its own atmosphere and story.

Carrel also draws inspiration from local game reserves and the people who protect these habitats. From the curve of a horn to the pattern of a coat, the goal is always the same: to create pieces that feel alive, honest, and timeless — artworks that connect collectors to the wild.

Conservation

A recurring theme in Carrel’s portfolio is the African wild dog (painted dog) — one of Africa’s most captivating and misunderstood predators. Through portrait-style studies and conservation-focused pieces, he highlights their intelligence, social bonds, and unique individuality.

The intention is simple: raise awareness through beauty. When people emotionally connect to a species, they’re more likely to care about its future. These works often spark conversations about habitat protection, responsible tourism, and the importance of preserving biodiversity.

Exhibitions & milestones

Carrel has travelled extensively — including to Tanzania — to exhibit and sell his work, meeting collectors and art lovers across the region. Alongside exhibitions, his paintings have been commissioned by clients from around the world, with requests ranging from signature big-cat portraits to highly personal wildlife pieces.

His creative process is strongly shaped by collaboration: working with African wildlife photographers for inspiration, partnering with local game reserves, and engaging with art galleries that support wildlife-focused storytelling. This mix of field influence and studio craft helps each painting carry authenticity — not just detail.
